Our Story

A Bridge Between Ancient Craft
and the Modern World

KalaSetu — "Kala" (art) + "Setu" (bridge) — exists to ensure that the extraordinary living traditions of India's tribal artisans are preserved, fairly compensated, and celebrated globally.

Our Mission

India's tribal communities carry knowledge systems thousands of years old. Dhokra metal casting, Warli painting, Gond art, Lambani embroidery — these are not merely handicrafts. They are living philosophies, oral histories embedded in form and colour.

Yet the artisans who carry these traditions are often underpaid, exploited by middlemen, and invisible to the world that buys their work. KalaSetu changes this by connecting artisans directly with conscious buyers — with full transparency, fair pricing, and cultural context.

Every purchase on KalaSetu flows directly to the artisan's community. Every product comes with the story of its tradition, verified by our network of cultural consultants.

What We Stand For

How Authenticity Verification Works

Our multi-step process ensures that every "Authentic" badge represents genuine craftsmanship rooted in a living tradition.

01

Artisan Submits

The artisan creates a detailed listing: high-resolution images, craft technique description, materials used, and the cultural tradition behind the piece.

02

Queue Review

The listing enters our consultant review queue. Assigned consultants are matched based on their area of expertise — e.g., a Gond art specialist reviews Gond submissions.

03

Expert Assessment

The consultant evaluates the technique, materials, and cultural claims. They may request additional information or images from the artisan.

04

Decision & Notes

Approved listings receive the "Authentic" badge along with cultural notes written by the consultant explaining the tradition. Rejected listings receive detailed feedback.
